Warranty Coverage

  • Products covered: This warranty applies to Luxury Interior Emulsion when used on properly prepared interior walls and ceilings (plaster, gypsum board, concrete, brick, fibre-cement, wood, masonry).
  • Warranty period: 8 years from purchase for residential or light-commercial use. 5 years for institutional or high-traffic areas (schools, hospitals, retail). Starts on purchase date for the original purchaser.
  • What we promise:
    • Adheres tightly to properly prepared and primed surfaces without peeling or blistering.
    • Resists cracking and flaking under normal substrate movement.
    • Maintains colour within acceptable tolerances without excessive fading or discolouration.
    • Remains washable and stain resistant when cleaned per Calyco care instructions.
    • Resists surface mould and fungi when used under normal humidity and ventilation.
  • If the coating fails as warranted, Calyco will provide replacement product for the affected area or refund the purchase price of the defective product. Labour costs are not covered.

Conditions for Warranty Validity

  • Surface suitability: Substrates must be sound, dry, clean, and free from contaminants. New masonry must cure at least 28 days and be below 15% moisture.
  • Surface preparation: Follow the applicable Calyco technical data sheet. Remove loose coatings; clean with mild detergent; treat fungus/algae; de-gloss shiny surfaces; apply the recommended Calyco primer or sealer.
  • Application conditions: Apply between 10°C and 40°C with relative humidity below 80%. Do not paint if rain or condensation is expected within four hours.
  • Application method: Use brush, roller, or airless spray suitable for water-borne coatings. Stir well. Use only clean water for dilution per product data sheet. Apply at least two full coats at the recommended spread rate; allow 3â€“4 hours between coats.
  • Maintenance and care: Avoid cleaning for two weeks post-application. Clean gently with mild soapy water; avoid abrasives or solvents. Maintain ventilation and manage moisture to prevent condensation or fungal growth.
  • Evidence of purchase and compliance: Retain invoice and batch numbers. Calyco may request photos, preparation records, or inspection reports during claim review.

What the Warranty Does Not Cover

  • Improper substrate or preparation, including damp or uncured masonry.
  • Structural or mechanical defects, movement, leaks, efflorescence, rising damp, or hydrostatic pressure.
  • Excess moisture or condensation leading to mould/mildew from poor ventilation or leaks.
  • Surface contact or abrasion damage, chemicals, graffiti, smoke, or abrasive cleaners.
  • External causes such as fire, flood, natural disasters, vandalism, or aggressive fumes.
  • Non-residential chemical exposure (strong acids, alkalis, solvents, bleaches).
  • Misuse or neglect, improper storage, over-dilution, or use of non-recommended tools.
  • Colour or sheen variations due to batch differences, application method, or lighting.
  • Normal ageing after the warranty period.
  • Incidental or consequential damages (labour, equipment, scaffolding, loss of use/revenue).

  • Except as stated, Calyco makes no other warranties, express or implied. Liability is limited to the purchase price of the product, subject to applicable law.

How to Make a Warranty Claim

  • Notify promptly: Inspect surfaces periodically. Notify Calyco within 30 days of discovering a defect.
  • Provide documentation: Send a written claim with invoice, product name, batch number, colour, photos, description, surface prep details, primer used, coat count, dilution, and environmental conditions.
  • Allow inspection: Calyco may inspect the site. If covered, Calyco will arrange the remedy; if not, we will explain the reason.
  • Other rights: This warranty gives specific legal rights; other rights may vary by jurisdiction.
